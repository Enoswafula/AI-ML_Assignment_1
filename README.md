# ⚽ International Football Match Analysis (1872–2026)

## 📌 Project Overview
This project explores historical international football match data spanning over 150 years. The goal is to uncover patterns in match outcomes, goal scoring behavior, and team performance using data analysis techniques.

The dataset contains over 49,000 international matches played between national teams worldwide.

---

## 📂 Dataset Description
The dataset includes the following key features:

- **Date** – The day the match was played  
- **Home Team** – The hosting team  
- **Away Team** – The visiting team  
- **Home Score** – Goals scored by the home team  
- **Away Score** – Goals scored by the away team  
- **Tournament** – Type of competition (e.g., Friendly, World Cup)  
- **City & Country** – Location of the match  
- **Neutral** – Indicates if the match was played at a neutral venue

## 🔍 Data Exploration

### Total Matches
The dataset contains **49,071 matches**, making it sufficiently large for meaningful analysis.

### Time Range
- **Earliest match:** 30th November 1872  
- **Latest match:** 26th January 2026  

This wide range allows for long-term trend analysis in international football.

### Number of Teams
There are **325 unique national teams** represented in the dataset.

This reflects the global expansion of football over time.

### Most Frequent Home Team
**Brazil** appears most frequently as a home team, with **610 matches**.

This suggests consistent participation and strong hosting presence in international football.

---

## ⚽ Goals Analysis

### Average Goals per Match
The average number of goals per match is approximately **2.6 goals**.

This confirms that football is generally a **low-scoring sport**, where matches are often decided by small margins.

### Highest Scoring Match
The dataset includes extreme high-scoring matches, typically involving unevenly matched teams.

These matches are rare but highlight disparities in team strength.

### Home vs Away Goals
Home teams have scored **more total goals** than away teams.

This suggests that playing at home provides a measurable advantage, likely due to:
- Familiarity with the environment  
- Crowd support  
- Reduced travel fatigue  

### Most Common Total Goals
Most matches have **low total goal counts**, typically between **0 and 3 goals**.

This reinforces the idea that football games are generally tight and competitive.

---

## 🏆 Match Outcomes Analysis

### Match Results Distribution
Analysis shows that:
- **Home wins** occur most frequently  
- **Away wins** occur less often  
- **Draws** are the least common outcome  

### Home Advantage
The data strongly supports the existence of **home advantage**:
- Home teams win more matches  
- Home teams score more goals  

This aligns with broader findings in sports analytics.

### Most Successful Teams
A small group of teams dominate in terms of total wins.

These teams have:
- Strong football infrastructure  
- Consistent performance over decades  
- High participation in international competitions
